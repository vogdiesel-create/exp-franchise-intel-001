How to Read a Franchise Disclosure Document Without Losing Your Mind

Buying a franchise is one of the biggest financial decisions most people make. The primary document you get to evaluate that decision? A 200-page legal document called the Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD). Most prospective franchisees either skip it entirely or hire a $5,000 franchise attorney to read it for them.

What Actually Matters in an FDD

  • Item 19 (Financial Performance) -- The only place franchisors share actual revenue/profit numbers. Not all franchisors include this.
  • Item 20 (Outlets) -- How many units opened, closed, and transferred. High closure rates are a red flag.
  • Item 3 (Litigation) -- Lawsuits between the franchisor and franchisees. Patterns here tell you everything.
  • Items 5-6 (Fees) -- Initial fees, ongoing royalties, advertising fees, technology fees. These add up fast.
  • Item 7 (Estimated Initial Investment) -- The true cost to open, including the numbers they bury in footnotes.

Why Most People Get This Wrong

Reading a single FDD in isolation tells you very little. The real value comes from comparing Item 19 numbers across competing franchises, tracking how Item 20 closure rates change year over year, and spotting patterns in Item 3 litigation.
